A modular kitchen is one of the biggest investments you'll make in your home. The right choice of materials, layout, and craftsman makes all the difference — especially in Indore's climate.

## Choosing the Right Layout

**L-shaped kitchens** work best for Indian homes with limited wall space. They give you efficient work triangles and room for an appliance corner.

**U-shaped kitchens** are ideal for larger kitchens and give maximum storage and counter space.

**Straight (parallel) kitchens** suit narrow galley-style kitchens common in apartments.

## Materials That Last in Indore

In Indore's heat, we recommend:
- **Marine-grade plywood** with laminate shutters — durable, moisture-resistant, budget-friendly.
- **BWP (Boiling Waterproof) ply** for areas near the sink and hob.
- **Soft-close hinges and channels** — the quality of hardware decides how your kitchen feels 5 years from now.
