People often use the words "carpenter" and "furniture contractor" interchangeably. They're related, but they're not the same — and the difference matters a lot when you're planning a kitchen, a full bedroom set, or an entire home interior.

## What a Carpenter Does

A carpenter is a skilled craftsman who works with wood. They can build furniture, fix a door, install a frame, or repair a broken hinge. A good carpenter is invaluable for specific, defined tasks.

But a carpenter typically works on what you tell them to — they build, they don't manage. If you need material, you arrange it. If there's a design decision, you make it. If something else needs to be coordinated (electrical points for the kitchen, tiling around the wardrobe), that's on you.

## What a Furniture Contractor Does

A furniture contractor handles the whole project. That includes:

- **Site visit and measurement** — they come to you, assess the space, and take proper dimensions
- **Design and planning** — they advise on layout, material options, and help you make decisions
- **Material sourcing** — they procure plywood, laminates, hardware, and fittings
- **Fabrication** — the actual building happens in their workshop or on-site
- **Installation** — they fit everything properly in your home
- **Warranty** — they stand behind the completed work

You deal with one team from start to finish. One point of contact. One warranty.

## Why the Difference Matters

If you hire a carpenter for a large project — a full modular kitchen, for example — you take on a lot of responsibility yourself. You need to know what materials to buy, where to buy them, what specs are correct, and how to coordinate everything. Most homeowners aren't equipped to do this, nor should they have to be.

A furniture contractor takes that entire burden off you. You describe what you need and what you want to spend. They plan, source, build, and install. When there's a problem, you call one number.

## When to Hire Which

**Hire a carpenter for:** Small repairs, a single custom shelf, door frame repair, minor modifications to existing furniture.

**Hire a furniture contractor for:** Modular kitchen, full bedroom set, wardrobes, office furniture, complete home interior, or anything that involves multiple pieces and significant investment.
