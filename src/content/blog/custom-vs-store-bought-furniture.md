Furniture shopping feels simple until you're standing in a showroom that has nothing in the right size, the right finish, or the right price. That's usually when people start asking — *should I just get it made?*

Here's a straightforward guide to help you decide.

## When Store-Bought Makes Sense

- **You need it fast.** Custom furniture takes 2–4 weeks for most pieces. If you're moving in next week, a store purchase makes sense.
- **It's a temporary home.** Renting for a year? Investing in custom furniture that won't move with you easily may not make financial sense.
- **It's a small, standard item.** A basic plastic stool or a simple shelf — sometimes off-the-shelf is perfectly fine.

## When Custom is Clearly Worth It

**Your space has non-standard dimensions.** Most Indian flats have walls that aren't quite the standard sizes that showroom furniture is designed for. Custom furniture fits your actual room — no gaps, no overhangs, no compromise.

**You're furnishing for the long term.** If you own your home and plan to stay, custom furniture made with quality materials will outlast anything you buy from a chain store by years — sometimes by a decade.

**Storage is critical.** Custom wardrobes, kitchen cabinets, and TV units can be designed to maximise every inch of your available space in a way that readymade simply cannot.

**You have specific requirements.** A kitchen designed around your cooking style, a wardrobe designed around your exact clothing collection, a study unit designed for your kids' books — this level of fit is only possible with custom.

## The Hidden Cost of "Cheaper" Store Furniture

Readymade furniture often looks affordable upfront. But consider:
- Flat-pack assembly weakens joints over time
- Cheaper particleboard swells and breaks in humidity
- Damaged parts are difficult or impossible to repair
- You often pay more for something that fits less well

A custom piece built from good plywood, with proper joinery, can be repaired, modified, and refinished years down the line. A flat-pack wardrobe usually can't.

## The Verdict

For anything that stays in your home long-term — kitchens, wardrobes, bedroom sets, TV units — custom is almost always the better investment. For small, temporary, or decorative pieces, store-bought is fine.
