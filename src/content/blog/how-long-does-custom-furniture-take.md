One of the most common questions people ask before commissioning custom furniture is: *"How long will it take?"* The honest answer is — it varies. But here's a realistic guide so you can plan properly.

## Why Custom Furniture Takes Time

Unlike readymade furniture sitting in a warehouse, custom furniture is built from scratch for your specific space. The process involves:

1. Site visit and measurement
2. Design discussion and approval
3. Material procurement
4. Fabrication in the workshop
5. Installation at your home

Each step takes time. Rushing any of them — especially fabrication or installation — is where quality problems come from.

## Typical Timelines

**Single furniture pieces (TV unit, single wardrobe, study table):** 7–12 days from confirmed order.

**Modular kitchen (straight or L-shaped):** 2–3 weeks from measurement approval.

**Full modular kitchen (U-shaped, complex layout, with loft units):** 3–4 weeks.

**Complete bedroom set (wardrobe + bed + dressing table):** 2–3 weeks.

**Full home interior (multiple rooms):** 6–10 weeks depending on scope, often done in phases.

These timelines assume material is available and there are no major design changes mid-project.

## What Can Delay a Project

**Last-minute design changes** — once material is cut and work has begun, changing the design costs time and sometimes material. Finalise your design before work starts.

**Unavailable material** — specific laminates, hardware, or ply grades occasionally need to be ordered. Your contractor should flag this upfront.

**Site not ready** — if civil work (plastering, tiling, electrical) isn't finished when the furniture team arrives to install, it delays everything. Plan furniture to start after civil work is complete.

## How to Plan Your Timeline

Work backwards from when you need the furniture ready. If you're moving into a new flat on a specific date, speak to your furniture contractor at least 6 weeks before that date for kitchens and full room setups.

For single pieces, 2–3 weeks of lead time is usually enough.

## A Word on Rushing

Every experienced contractor has seen what happens when furniture is rushed — uneven finishes, loose fittings, hardware installed incorrectly. Quality work takes the time it takes. If a contractor promises to build your full modular kitchen in 4 days, ask questions.
