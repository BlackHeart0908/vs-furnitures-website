A good carpenter can transform your home. A bad one can cost you double what you budgeted and leave you with furniture that falls apart in two years. The difference often isn't obvious upfront — so here's how to tell them apart before you sign anything.

## Start With Word of Mouth

The most reliable way to find a good carpenter in Indore is through someone who has already used them. Ask neighbours, family, colleagues — specifically ask to *see* the work if possible. Furniture is something you can inspect in person, and a contractor who does great work is usually happy for you to visit a past client.

Referrals are powerful because the contractor's reputation is on the line with someone you both know.

## What to Check Before Hiring

**1. Do they visit your site before quoting?**
Any serious furniture contractor should visit your home, take measurements, and then give you a quote. Be cautious of anyone who quotes a price over the phone without seeing the space.

**2. Do they give a written estimate?**
A verbal quote is worth nothing. Insist on a written breakdown — materials, labour, hardware, timeline. This protects both sides.

**3. Do they offer a warranty — in writing?**
The most important question. A contractor who stands behind their work will give you a written warranty without hesitation. If someone avoids this question, walk away.

**4. How long have they been operating?**
Experience matters in carpentry. A team that has been building kitchens and wardrobes in Indore for 10–20+ years has seen every type of home, every type of problem, and knows what works. Ask how long they have been in business and ask for examples of past work.

**5. Do they handle the full project or subcontract?**
Some contractors take your order and subcontract the work to cheaper, unknown teams. Ask directly: *"Will your own team be doing all the work?"* One team, one point of accountability, is always better.

## Red Flags to Watch For

- Quotes significantly lower than everyone else (usually means quality cuts somewhere)
- Unwillingness to put anything in writing
- Asking for very large advance payments upfront
- Vague timelines ("we'll finish when it's done")
- No previous work to show you
