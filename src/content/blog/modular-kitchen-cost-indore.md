The most common question we get before a free site visit is: "What will my modular kitchen cost?" The honest answer is it depends — but there is a wide range of "it depends" and this guide will help you understand exactly where your kitchen is likely to fall.

## Price by Kitchen Layout

The layout is the single biggest cost driver. More linear metres of cabinetry means more material, more hardware, and more labour.

| Kitchen Layout | Estimated Price Range | Best For |
|---|---|---|
| Straight / One-wall | ₹1.2 lakh – ₹2.2 lakh | Small flats, compact spaces |
| L-Shaped | ₹2 lakh – ₹4 lakh | Most standard Indore flats |
| Parallel / Galley | ₹2 lakh – ₹3.5 lakh | Narrow kitchens with two walls |
| U-Shaped | ₹3.5 lakh – ₹6 lakh | Larger kitchens, maximum storage |
| Island Kitchen | ₹4.5 lakh – ₹8 lakh+ | Open-plan homes |

These are complete kitchen costs including carcass, shutters, hardware, loft units, and countertop sourcing assistance. They do not include civil work (breaking walls, electrical points, plumbing), which is handled separately.

## What Drives the Cost Up

**1. Kitchen size**
The most obvious factor. A 7-foot straight kitchen costs roughly half of a 14-foot L-shaped one. We measure your actual kitchen before giving an estimate — layout type alone does not determine cost.

**2. Shutter finish**
The visible face of every cabinet. From lowest to highest cost:
- High-pressure laminate (HPL) — most common, most durable, wide range of finishes
- Acrylic (high-gloss) — more expensive, premium look, shows fingerprints
- PU (Polyurethane) painted finish — smooth, satin or matte, higher cost than HPL
- Veneer — real wood grain, premium pricing

For most Indore kitchens, laminate gives the best value. The difference between a good laminate and acrylic shutters on a standard L-shaped kitchen can be ₹40,000 to ₹80,000.

**3. Hardware brand**
Hinges and drawer channels are the parts you touch every day. Quality hardware from Hettich, Hafele, or Blum costs more but lasts 10+ years without loosening or squeaking. Local-brand hardware is significantly cheaper but often fails within 2 to 3 years. We recommend Hettich or equivalent on all our kitchens — the cost premium is small relative to the lifespan difference.

**4. Internal fittings**
Tandem boxes (deep internal drawers), pull-out baskets, bottle pullouts, corner carousel units, and magic corners all add cost but significantly increase usability. A kitchen with well-designed internal fittings is far more practical than one with more shutter area but no internal organisation.

**5. Countertop material**
We help source and fit the countertop as part of the project. Granite is the most popular choice in Indore — it is extremely hard, heat-resistant, scratch-resistant, and available locally. Quartz is more uniform in appearance and slightly higher cost. Corian and engineered stone are available at premium pricing. Budget ₹15,000 to ₹40,000 for a standard countertop depending on stone type and kitchen size.

**6. Tall units and loft units**
Tall pantry units and loft units above the counter maximise vertical storage — essential in Indian kitchens where storage is almost always the top complaint. These add to the total cost but are nearly always worth the investment.

## What Drives the Cost Down

**1. Fewer internal fittings**
Keeping internals simple — fixed shelves instead of pull-out baskets, standard hinged doors instead of tandem drawers — reduces cost meaningfully. This is the right call if budget is tight, because the carcass quality and hardware brand matter more than fancy internals.

**2. Laminate over acrylic or PU**
Choosing good-quality HPL laminate over acrylic or PU finish shutter saves money without compromising durability. In a working kitchen, a matte or satin laminate is often more practical than a high-gloss finish that shows every fingerprint and steam mark.

**3. Straight or L-shaped over U-shaped**
If your kitchen space allows a straight or L-shaped layout, this will always cost less than a U-shape. We advise on the most practical layout during the site visit.

## Materials Breakdown — What You Are Paying For

| Component | Material We Use | Why It Matters |
|---|---|---|
| Carcass (main body) | BWP / BWR Plywood | Waterproof — survives Indore humidity and heat. Lasts 15–20 years. |
| Shutters | Laminate, Acrylic, or PU | Visible face — determines look and durability |
| Hinges & Channels | Hettich or equivalent | Daily use hardware — soft-close, lasts 10+ years |
| Countertop | Granite or Quartz | Hardest work surface — heat and scratch resistant |
| Loft backing | Same ply as carcass | Ensures loft units are as durable as base units |

One thing worth emphasising: the carcass material is hidden once the kitchen is installed. There is no visual difference between a BWP plywood carcass and a particle board carcass after installation. But in 5 to 7 years, the difference is dramatic — BWP plywood stays solid while particle board swells, sags, and deteriorates, especially near sinks and appliances. Always ask what the carcass material is.

## How to Budget Your Indore Modular Kitchen

**Step 1: Know your kitchen size.** Measure the length of all walls where you plan to put cabinets. This is the most important input for any estimate.

**Step 2: Decide on a layout.** We help with this during the site visit — we will suggest the most practical layout for your space and cooking habits.

**Step 3: Set a realistic budget range.** For a standard Indore 2BHK flat with an L-shaped kitchen, budget ₹2.5 to ₹3.5 lakh for a well-made kitchen with good materials and hardware. Trying to do the same kitchen for ₹1.2 lakh means compromising seriously on either materials, hardware, or warranty.

**Step 4: Book a free site visit.** Every kitchen estimate requires an in-person visit and measurement. We offer this for free with no obligation anywhere within 7 km of Indore.

## What Is Not Included in a Kitchen Quote

To avoid surprises, here is what kitchen contractors typically quote separately:

- **Civil work** — breaking tiles, plastering, relocating plumbing or gas points
- **Electrical points** — adding plug points inside cabinets or under loft units
- **Chimney and hob** — appliances are usually sourced by the client; we can advise on specifications
- **Sink and tap** — most clients source these; we can fit them as part of the installation
- **Painting** — wall painting after kitchen installation is handled separately
